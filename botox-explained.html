Is Botox the same as anti-wrinkle injections?

A clear, clinician-led explanation — without jargon or hype.

What do people usually mean by “Botox”?

Many people use the word Botox as a general term to describe anti-wrinkle injections. In everyday conversation, it has become shorthand for treatments that soften expression lines caused by muscle movement.

Clinically, however, Botox is a brand name. In medical practice, clinicians use prescription-only medicines that work in a similar way, and the specific product used is chosen based on clinical assessment rather than branding.

Why clinics often avoid brand-led language

In the UK, anti-wrinkle injections are classed as prescription-only medicines. For this reason, responsible clinics focus on the treatment and outcome, rather than advertising specific brand names.

This approach keeps the emphasis on safety, suitability, and personalised care — not on a one-size-fits-all product.

What anti-wrinkle injections actually do

Anti-wrinkle injections work by temporarily relaxing specific muscles that cause dynamic lines, such as frown lines, forehead lines, and crow’s feet.

  • Results develop gradually over 3–14 days
  • Expressions remain natural when dosing is appropriate
  • Effects typically last around 3–4 months

Treatment is always tailored to your facial anatomy, muscle strength, and goals.
